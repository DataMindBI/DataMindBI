# 02-brand/

**Purpose**: Brand guidelines, visual identity, messaging framework, and tone standards. The authoritative source for "how we express ourselves."

## What Belongs Here

- Brand guidelines and brand book
- Logo usage and visual identity standards
- Color palette and typography
- Tone and voice guidelines
- Messaging framework and key messages
- Brand positioning statement
- Visual assets (logos, templates, icons)
- Brand storytelling and narrative

## What Does NOT Belong Here

- ❌ Company strategy (see 01-company/)
- ❌ Service definitions (see 04-services/)
- ❌ Website page content (see 05-website/)
- ❌ Product roadmaps (see 07-products/)
- ❌ Marketing content (see 06-market/)
- ❌ Methodology (see 03-methodology/)

## Approval Authority

**Kenny Bush** — Principal  
All brand decisions require explicit approval to ensure consistency with company positioning.

## Document Format

All documents use YAML front matter with document_id, version, status, owner, reviewer, approved_by, approved_date.

See `/templates/document-template.md` for structure.

## Creating Brand Documents

1. Use the appropriate template from `/templates/`
2. Fill in YAML front matter
3. Mark status as `draft`
4. Create feature branch
5. Open draft PR with:
   - Brand rationale
   - Connection to company positioning
   - Impact on market perception
6. Kenny approves

## Updating Brand Standards

Brand decisions can evolve, but always:
1. Document the rationale for change
2. Increment version number
3. Mark status as `in_review`
4. Get Kenny's approval
5. Communicate changes to all teams

## Using Brand Standards

**For DataMind team members**: Follow approved brand guidelines in all internal and external communications.

**For implementation repositories** (e.g., datamind-website): Reference approved brand standards when:
- Designing website layouts or campaigns
- Creating marketing materials
- Defining user-facing copy or imagery
- Building brand collateral
