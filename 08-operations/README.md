# 08-operations/

**Purpose**: Operating standards, company culture, and how we work. The authoritative source for "how DataMind operates internally."

## What Belongs Here

- Company values and culture
- Operating principles (how we make decisions, collaborate, handle conflicts)
- Communication standards and protocols
- Work practices and norms (async/sync, meeting standards, documentation)
- Team structure and roles
- Onboarding and offboarding procedures
- Professional development and learning standards
- Hiring and interview standards
- Compensation and benefits philosophy
- Budgeting and resource allocation processes

## What Does NOT Belong Here

- ❌ HR details or sensitive personnel information
- ❌ Brand guidelines (see 02-brand/)
- ❌ Company strategy (see 01-company/)
- ❌ Service definitions (see 04-services/)
- ❌ Methodology (see 03-methodology/)
- ❌ Project management or PMO (that's DataMind Forge)

## Approval Authority

**Kenny Bush** — Principal  
All operating standards require Kenny's explicit approval.

## Document Format

All documents use YAML front matter with document_id, version, status, owner, reviewer, approved_by, approved_date.

See `/templates/document-template.md` for structure.

## Creating Operating Standards

1. Use `/templates/document-template.md`
2. Define:
   - The standard or practice
   - Why it matters for DataMind
   - Who follows it and when
   - Examples and edge cases
3. Mark status as `draft`
4. Create feature branch
5. Open draft PR with clear rationale
6. Kenny approves

## Updating Operating Standards

When practices evolve:
1. Increment version number
2. Mark status as `in_review`
3. Document the change and rationale
4. Get Kenny's approval
5. Communicate to all team members

## Relationship to DataMind Forge

DataMind Forge may enforce operating standards by:
- Checking that work practices follow approved standards
- Verifying that team communication aligns with company values
- Tracking compliance with organizational procedures
